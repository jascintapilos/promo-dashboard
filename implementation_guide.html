Week 1: Launch (Jun 9–13)

Step 1: Share Documents with Sales Team Lead

  • Email all 5 documents (or link to index.html)
  • Schedule 30-min sync for Jun 12 to walk through Tier 1/2/3
  • Have them sign off on Decision Framework checklist

Step 2: Brief Wai Yip

  • Email the Gap Closure Summary + all docs
  • Explain: This reduces routine Qs; frees 20–25 hrs/week for strategy
  • Get commitment to 4-week pilot + weekly Friday syncs (3 PM)
  • Confirm Tier 3 definition is what he expects

Step 3: Configure Existing #ba-promo Channel

  • Update channel topic: "Post Tier 2 policy decisions here → 2–4hr response time. NO DMs to @Wai Yip."
  • Pin the Decision Framework (link or summary)
  • Pin posting format guidelines for Tier 2 requests

Week 2: Launch Weekly List (Jun 12–20)

Step 1: Generate First Weekly Promo List

  • Promo team extracts active promos from BO (WS1, WS2, QPRO1, QP2A)
  • Fill in Weekly Active Promo List template with all bonus codes, terms, tiers
  • Add notes on quota limits, new rules, expired promos
  • Post to #ba-promo Monday Jun 12, 10:30 AM

Step 2: Monitor #ba-promo Questions

  • Track all Tier 2 questions posted
  • Answer within 2–4 hours (target: 100% within timeframe)
  • Note: Sales team should ask here, NOT DM Wai Yip
  • Wai Yip: Do NOT respond to DMs about routine promos (enforce policy)

Step 3: Friday Sync (Jun 14, 3 PM)

  • Jascinta + Wai Yip review first week
  • Track: How many Tier 2 Qs posted? How many escalated to Wai Yip?
  • Check: Are questions being answered in 2–4 hours?
  • Celebrate: Sales team asking Tier 2 Qs instead of DM'ing Wai Yip

Weeks 3–4: Stabilize (Jun 23–Jul 4)

Step 1: Coach Sales Team Lead on Tier 1 Gaps

  • Jascinta reviews #ba-promo questions
  • If any Tier 1 questions appear → coach sales team lead on the framework
  • Pattern example: "Player always asks 'does this player qualify?'—that's a Tier 1 checklist item"

Step 2: Confirm Tier 3 <2 Per Week

  • Track escalations to Wai Yip
  • Target: <2 escalations per week (down from 10+)
  • If escalations still high, investigate: Are Tier 1/2 filtering working?

Step 3: Weekly Sync (Every Friday, 3 PM)

  • Jascinta + Wai Yip review week's activity
  • Track metrics: Tier 2 Qs, Tier 3 escalations, decision speed
  • Confirm next week's Weekly Promo List is ready

Week 5: Graduation (Jul 7–9)

Graduation Review Checklist

  • Sales team lead makes Tier 1 decisions without prompting (≥80%)
  • Zero Tier 1/2 questions escalated to Wai Yip
  • Wai Yip receives <2 Tier 3 escalations per week
  • Weekly Promo List consistently used by sales
  • Sales team lead explains 5 Principles from memory
  • #ba-promo is default Q&A channel (not DMs)

If All Checks Pass ✅

Declare victory! Sales team lead autonomy achieved. Shift to maintenance mode (weekly promo list + monitoring).

If Some Checks Fail ❌

Extend coaching: Identify gaps, coach through mid-July, re-review Jul 23.

Risk Mitigation

Risk 1: Sales Team Lead Keeps DM'ing Wai Yip

Mitigation: Wai Yip does NOT respond to DMs. Redirect to #ba-promo. Jascinta coaches sales team lead on Slack norms.

Risk 2: Weekly Promo List Gets Outdated

Mitigation: Automate if possible (pull from BO API). Set calendar reminder. Sales team knows to ask #ba-promo if stale.

Risk 3: Sales Team Misses Compliance/Fraud Issues

Mitigation: Tier 3 includes compliance. Wai Yip still reviews high-risk assignments. Coaching log tracks close calls.

Risk 4: Decision Framework Doesn't Cover Edge Cases

Mitigation: Tier 2 (#ba-promo) is the "ask for help" layer. Update framework quarterly based on patterns.
