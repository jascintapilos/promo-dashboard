# Promo Value Creation

- **Project ID:** PVC-2026-01
- **Status:** Initiation / management review
- **Executive visibility:** HOD and CEO
- **Lead function:** Promotion
- **Planning horizon:** July–December 2026

## Executive purpose

Build a measurable operating model that helps Promotion improve campaign
effectiveness, control bonus cost, and support incremental revenue—while
keeping campaign-source accountability with the originating business teams.

The project moves Promotion beyond request execution into structured planning,
quality control, cost governance, and performance review. It does not transfer
campaign origination or commercial ownership to Promotion.

## Business goals

### Goal A — Increase promotion effectiveness

- Introduce calendar-based planning around agreed commercial moments.
- Standardise campaign briefs so objective, segment, timing, mechanics, and
  success measures are clear before setup.
- Pilot segment-triggered campaigns using source-team criteria and player lists.

### Goal B — Decrease promo cost

- Test the minimum effective bonus instead of assuming a larger reward performs
  better.
- Audit bonus caps against claimant deposits and actual cap utilisation.
- Reduce duplicate, outdated, low-uptake, and abuse-prone offers.

### Goal C — Increase revenue contribution

- Prioritise reactivation because the business already knows these players,
  their previous behaviour, and the channels available to reach them. This
  makes the first pilot faster and more measurable than acquisition.
- Pilot cross-product bridging to move existing players into another relevant
  product without paying full acquisition cost again.
- Build attribution in stages; report NGR only after the attribution method is
  validated and approved.

## Scope and accountability

| Area | Promotion owns | Source team owns |
|---|---|---|
| Campaign brief | Completeness check and clarification | Objective, audience, business rationale, priority |
| Planning | Calendar coordination and execution readiness | Commercial timing and campaign demand |
| Mechanics | Configuration review, feasibility, QC | Budget and expected player action |
| Targeting | Applying approved criteria and lists | Segment definition and eligible-player source |
| Execution | Setup, testing, launch, monitoring | Channel/business coordination where applicable |
| Measurement | Operational, claim, bonus-cost and deposit reporting | Commercial interpretation and follow-up action |
| Attribution | Evidence preparation | Agreement with Data/BI and management on methodology |

### In scope

- Campaign request quality gate and standard brief
- Monthly promo calendar
- Segment-triggered campaign pilots
- Reactivation and cross-product pilots
- Minimum-effective-bonus testing
- Promo cap, duplicate, sunset, and abuse reviews
- Pre-live QC and an **Issues & Follow-up Log**
- Post-campaign review and management reporting
- Data-source mapping and CSIR connection QC

### Out of scope until separately approved

- Promotion independently originating all campaigns
- Reporting NGR as caused by a promo before attribution is validated
- Full automation scaling before control and QC gates pass
- Permanent cap or offer changes without the required approval

## Executive quality gates

| Gate | Evidence required | Decision |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Project mandate | Scope, ownership boundary, goals and success measures | HOD confirms the operating model |
| 2. Data readiness | Source owners, campaign codes, definitions and completeness check | Data is sufficient for the selected pilot |
| 3. Pilot readiness | Audience, mechanics, budget, exclusions, QC and baseline | HOD approves controlled launch |
| 4. Results review | Claims, cost, deposits, issues and limitations | Continue, adjust, or stop |
| 5. Scale decision | Repeatable uplift, controlled cost and stable process | Management approves wider rollout |

No initiative moves to the next stage with an unresolved critical gate.

## Roadmap

| Stage | Focus | Main outputs |
|---|---|---|
| Current — baseline and approval | Confirm ownership, definitions, gaps and priorities | Charter, discovery workbook, decision log |
| First 30 days — control build | Standard brief, calendar, QC, cap sample, reactivation design | Approved templates, audit sample, two pilot plans |
| Next 30–60 days — controlled pilots | Reactivation, segment campaign and cross-product test | Pilot results and post-mortems |
| September–December — validate and scale | Attribution, dashboard, repeatable operating rhythm | Scale recommendation and executive scorecard |

## Success measures

| Goal | Primary measures |
|---|---|
| Effectiveness | First-pass brief completeness, planned-vs-reactive ratio, claim rate, deposit conversion |
| Cost | Bonus cost, cap vs deposit, cap utilisation, cost per claim, cost per reactivated player |
| Revenue | Deposit within the agreed window, reactivated players, cross-product conversion |
| Control | QC pass rate, repeat issue rate, actions closed on time |

NGR remains a Phase 3 measure and must not be presented as promo-attributed
revenue until the attribution model passes the relevant quality gate.

## Current evidence baseline

- 179 valid WS1 offer assignments are available for January–March 2026.
- 1,439 approved WS1 executions were recovered for April–23 July 2026.
- The April–July execution data covers 592 unique members and MYR 657,328 in
  bonus cost.
- April–July assignment denominators are not yet available, so claim conversion
  for that period cannot be presented as complete.
- The CSIR connection passed the approved tunnel, authentication, read-only,
  schema, and bounded-data checks on 23 July 2026.

## Immediate management decisions

1. Confirm the project mandate and the boundary between campaign source and
   Promotion execution.
2. Approve the standard campaign brief and request quality gate.
3. Approve the cap-audit and offer-sunset governance process.
4. Select one reactivation pilot and one segment/cross-product pilot.
5. Nominate the source-team and Data/BI owners for post-campaign data and
   attribution.
